# Creative Direction: Texas Iron × Brutalist Opera

Version: 0.1 direction draft\
Visual north star: **Texas iron × brutalist opera**\
Lighting hierarchy: **tungsten over neon**

## 1. The equation

> **Texas iron is the scale of work. Brutalist opera is the scale of civilization. Storybook salvage is the emotional filter. Tungsten is the light of people keeping things alive.**

The result is a sun-struck machine country rebuilding civic life inside monumental ruins: weighty, strange, funny, and worth saving.

- **Texas iron** supplies cast housings, plate steel, oilfield skids, rail hardware, water tanks, corrugated shade, cable, canvas, toolboxes, service vehicles, and things repaired with leverage.
- **Brutalist opera** supplies monoliths, deep apertures, cantilevers, ceremonial stairs, acoustic chambers, axial approaches, floodlit stages, civic banners, and machinery treated as public ritual.
- **Storybook salvage** keeps the place humane: readable silhouettes, affection, hand-painted interventions, gardens, patched cloth, jokes, rituals, and accumulated history.
- **Tungsten over neon** makes inhabited maintenance warm and visible while reserving colored light for rare signals, legacy systems, factions, hazards, or commerce.

Player-built life occupies, braces, illuminates, and humanizes inherited monumentality. The ruins provide awe; the colony provides affection. Ordinary construction remains modular and repairable; monumental concrete belongs mainly to inherited infrastructure, landmarks, and institutions.

These references are internal design coordinates, not asset prompts or copying licenses.

| Reference | Assigned job | Translate into | Do not take |
|---|---|---|---|

| Atomic Heart | Art-direction ambition | monumental institutions, machines as sculpture, precision automation made uncanny, confident contrast | Soviet iconography, polymer, signature robots, statues, surface motifs |

Production briefs and generated-asset prompts use the abstracted grammar only. A reference-removal review must find no recognizable borrowed silhouette, prop, interface, icon, character, building, or motif.

## 3. Visual layers

| Layer | Form | Material | Motion | Emotional job |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| World bones | monoliths, dams, viaducts, retaining walls, deep voids | board-formed concrete, weathering steel, aggregate | rare, slow, immense | awe and historical weight |
| Working city | low, wide, braced, bolted, modular | cast iron, mill-scale steel, galvanized sheet, rubber, timber | pumps, belts, cranes, shutters | competence and legibility |
| Lived layer | shade, rails, signs, gardens, domestic additions | enamel, canvas, cloth, ceramic, reused wood | laundry, fans, residents, service routines | affection and civic identity |
| Precision relics | smooth, sparse, sculptural, unusually complete | pale ceramic, polished alloy, dark glass | quiet, exact, choreographed | wonder and unease |
| Faction intervention | doctrine-specific restructuring | construction and repair logic, not color alone | distinct work rhythms | a philosophy made physical |

At gameplay distance the read order is **mass → function → operating state → history**. A major city improvement must change skyline, working rhythm, light map, civic routine, or several of these—not merely add decoration.

Western cues are infrastructural, not costume shorthand: water rights, rail, ranch and oilfield hardware, reservoir works, wind fences, roadside engineering, service yards, heat, distance, and weather. Avoid ubiquitous stars, longhorns, saloons, hats, skulls, spikes, and improvised armor cosplay.

## 4. Architecture

### Inherited civic monument

- deep shade and large legible shadow masses;
- axial approaches, framed voids, processional stairs, and proscenium-like work spaces;
- concrete sized for a more confident civilization than the one that survives;
- integrated machine halls, spillways, rail cuts, reservoirs, towers, and service tunnels;
- precision remnants used sparingly enough to remain uncanny.

### Settlement intervention

- reversible braces, gantries, platforms, awnings, ducts, pipes, cables, stairs, and welded rooms;
- structures explain how residents reach, feed, maintain, and escape machinery;
- domestic life colonizes hard infrastructure through light, shade, cloth, paint, plants, sound, and personal objects;
- growth reveals choices: export town, water sanctuary, repair hub, caravan crossroads, human community, robot community, or mixed civic experiment.

### Brutalist opera staging

Grandeur comes from composition rather than detail density: controlled approach, compression and release, immense machinery, rhythmic repetition, acoustic volume, banners or work lights, and a clear human-scale intervention. Gameplay spaces retain entrances, exits, work targets, road access, and status readability.

## 5. Materials and wear

Core families:

- mill-scale plate steel, with broad roughness and manufacturing variation;
- cast iron with heavy section thickness and rounded transitions;
- board-formed concrete with form ties, aggregate, runoff, and structural cracks;
- chipped enamel showing topcoat, primer, then metal in causal order;
- galvanized sheet with restrained directional variation;
- heavy rubber, canvas, ceramic, oily timber, dusty glass, cable, and hose;
- rare pale ceramic, polished alloy, and dark glass for precision relics.

Wear answers a cause:

- rust collects at water traps, cuts, fasteners, and dissimilar-metal joins;
- polish follows handles, steps, cable paths, and service access;
- soot follows exhaust and hot airflow;
- dust follows wind and upward exposure;
- mineral traces follow pipes and spillways;
- repair paint surrounds real intervention.

Random edge wear, uniform grunge, generic brown wash, and decals without history remain forbidden.

### Day

- hard, bleached sunlight; deep useful shade; warm dust bounce;
- mineral and neutral mass dominates;
- iron, dust, water, paint, cloth, and plants carry localized identity;
- strong emissive color remains exceptional.

### Night

- darkness and cool environmental fill remain the majority;
- warm practical pools identify occupied, maintained, and safe space;
- home becomes visibly warmer as it heals;
- neon/emissive color is generally under five percent of the frame and never becomes nightlife wallpaper;
- bloom stays restrained and UI remains readable.

Tungsten practicals in the approximate `2200–3000K` family are the inhabited baseline. Amber means care, work, service, gathering, or a maintained route. Colored neon means specific legacy automation, faction claim, precision hazard, or surviving commerce. It never carries gameplay state alone. Every night composition must pass grayscale, no-bloom, and emissive-disabled checks.

## 7. Vehicles, factories, and the exchange

- Vehicles resemble maintained field-service machines: wide stance, visible suspension, recovery points, cargo geometry, job-specific tool silhouettes, and accumulated repairs.
- Factories read as civic organs. Inputs arrive, work moves through visible stages, outputs occupy real storage, and failures change motion, sound, smoke, and light.
- Manufacturing progression replaces scavenged improvisation with standards without making the city sterile.
- The caravan exchange is a physical stockyard beneath monumental infrastructure: loading gantries, certified lots, weigh stations, clerks, route boards, warehouses, warm work lights, and rare colored legacy signs.
- Market abundance changes traffic, storage, skyline, noise, labor routines, and faction presence. It cannot exist only as UI.

## 8. Camera and staging

- Preserve the provisional 35–45° strategy-camera elevation and 35–45° perspective FOV until D-0022 testing.
- City views favor stable horizon logic, roof silhouettes, operating states, and visible logistics paths.
- Road cameras emphasize wheel contact, suspension weight, tool reach, cargo, and horizon—not speed blur.
- Encounters keep the relevant vehicle, object, faction representative, and exit route readable together.
- Cinematic punctuation is brief, usually two to five seconds, and returns control with authoritative state visible.
- No routine Dutch angles, gameplay depth of field, camera shake masking weak handling, or beauty lighting that contradicts play.

## 9. Story shape

Stories follow the game’s causal structure:

```text
civic need → ambitious practical scheme → visible opportunity cost
→ encounter with another way of living → persistent civic consequence
```

The city, road, manufacturing, exchange, and factions tell the same story. Dialogue explains character and competing interpretations; state changes deliver the consequence. Sasha is provisionally observant and capable, not a nonstop quip system. Exact voice, background, embodiment, and civic authority remain open.

## 10. Comedy constitution

Comedy should make the player like the colony more. It comes through:

1. **Systems** — a technically valid solution creates an absurd civic side effect.
2. **Characters** — incompatible priorities produce specificity, not generic banter.
3. **Environment** — original signs, forms, relic institutions, and retrofits reveal how people lived.
4. **Escalation** — Sasha’s practical plan collides with weather, bureaucracy, faction doctrine, or the vehicle’s physical limits.

Satirize institutions, incentives, status, and grand promises—not poverty, disability, trauma, or survival needs. Robots do not default to “literal machine misunderstands feelings,” and humans are not automatically chaotic foils. A good joke leaves a trace: nickname, sign, object, policy, queue entry, relationship flag, or changed routine. Do not joke over irreversible loss or a crisis’s emotional peak. Avoid memes, topical brand analogues, exposition disguised as jokes, one universal sarcastic voice, and dialogue trees that displace the city/road loop.

## 11. Sound and music direction

The acoustic world should carry the same hierarchy:

- machinery establishes rhythm through pumps, belts, impacts, relays, fans, cable tension, engines, and distant rail;
- inhabited areas add voices, tools, radios, cloth, water, service rituals, and music;
- brutalist spaces provide long reflections and scale; workshops remain close, dry, and intelligible;
- tungsten spaces sound warm and occupied; precision relics sound controlled, sparse, and slightly too exact;
- score may combine industrial rhythm, chamber/orchestral scale, and regionally grounded instrumentation only after authentic collaborators define the cultural language.

“Opera” means staging, scale, recurrence, and emotional architecture—not constant choir or musical bombast.

## 12. The Last Bearing first-look

- The failing turbine sits in a raw-concrete spillway like an exhausted civic organ; amber service lamps fail in sequence while one small legacy gauge remains precisely colored.
- Low iron workshops, tanks, shade structures, and domestic additions occupy the monument’s base.
- The machine shop is an oilfield-service shed under a concrete cantilever; its crane reads like stage rigging.
- Sasha’s vehicle is a broad field-service chassis with visible suspension, recovery points, cargo, and one unmistakable tool silhouette.
- The road crosses bleached industrial hinterland, rail cuts, reservoir works, and wind fencing while a dust front consumes distant monuments.
- The transit depot is a brutalist waiting hall used as the encounter proscenium; portable warm worklights reveal who maintains it.
- The faction claim appears through rigging, barriers, service markings, temporary power, or a maintenance ritual—not a palette swap.
- The return changes at least one skyline element, light pattern, routine, and sound layer.

A provisional comic beat may turn an obsolete claims office into painfully formal salvage bureaucracy. The procedure is funny; the faction’s need for the bearing stays legitimate.

## 13. Golden-direction gates

### Slice direction gate (C0–C2)

Before vertical-slice asset fan-out, approve:

1. one midday settlement frame;
2. the same settlement at tungsten-led night;
3. one road/vehicle frame;
4. one brutalist inherited landmark occupied by modular settlement life;
5. one human, one original humanoid utility robot, and one mixed-work scene;
6. one state-dependent Last Bearing comedy/story beat.

### Manufacturing direction gate (C3)

Before manufacturing-asset fan-out, approve one factory input-to-output state sheet showing physical inputs, work, output, storage, stoppage, light, motion, and sound.

### Caravan Exchange direction gate (C4)

Before exchange-asset fan-out, approve one caravan stockyard dusk/night frame showing physical lots, loading, administration, route information, tungsten-led work light, and rare semantic neon.

Each applicable gate must pass normal gameplay camera with representative UI, grayscale, no-bloom, emissive-disabled, silhouette, material-causality, faction-without-color, reference-removal, and target-Mac performance reviews. A later C3/C4 frame is not a prerequisite or authorization for C2 work. No beauty render can overrule a failed gameplay frame.

## 14. Phased art production

- **C0 — Direction proof:** grayscale compositions, palette keys, material balls, lighting wedges, one story/comedy tone test.
- **C1 — Golden kit:** water building, workshop, field-service vehicle, human/robot pair, concrete world bone, road kit.
- **C2 — Vertical-slice language:** depot, first faction intervention, weather, return transformation, sound and UI skeleton.
- **C3 — Manufacturing language:** visible production, warehouses, grades, packaging, loading, factory night states.
- **C4 — Caravan exchange:** stockyard institution, caravan identity, regional goods, trade rituals, market story beats.
- **C5 — Faction expansion:** doctrine-specific construction, repair, movement, light, sound, and comedy matrices.

Every phase begins with a tiny governed brief and ends with creator acceptance. The C1 golden kit and reference-removal tests are required before any fan-out; asset agents then scale within C2, C3, or C4 only after that phase's applicable direction gate passes.

## 15. Open calibration choices

- fictional Texas composite: oilfield, rail, and reservoir interior basin is the recommendation;
- undated layered industrial archaeology rather than a fixed alternate-history year;
- monumental brutalism concentrated in world bones and institutions;
- neon used first for legacy systems and faction claims;
- precision technology kept rare;
- Sasha written with dry, warm competence until voice tests;
- satire aimed first at survival bureaucracy and failed civic/technology promises;
- humor placed before and after pressure, with silence during genuine loss;
- first faction doctrine selected only when its mechanics, depot claim, art kit, and comic voice can be designed together.

These are recommended calibrations, not creator-ratified facts.
