# Proof-Driven Build Roadmap

Version: 0.1 draft\
Scheduling principle: calendar commitments follow measured engine and asset throughput, not reference-game ambition.

## M0T — Authorize the isolated technical proof

Deliverables:

- choose repository location while autonomy remains A0;
- explicitly accept WP-0001 before any narrowly scoped A1 experiment;
- approve the exact Unity installation;
- establish the disposable A1 branch/worktree boundary in Section 8 of the trust document, pinned to a creator-approved base and with manual diff/import.

Exit: the reversible gameplay-neutral technical spike has explicit creator authorization and an actual quarantine boundary. It may start without silently ratifying the game's design center.

## M0G — Ratify the gameplay center

Deliverables:

- resolve the exact gameplay-graybox entry constraints in `governance/ratification-state.json`;
- revise and ratify the thesis, constitutional loop, P1–P6 core, travel topology, and product boundary;
- preserve the ratified Texas iron × brutalist opera / tungsten-over-neon north star and translate the provisional reference matrix into original styleframes and tone tests;
- authorize—not pre-decide—the city-grammar comparison;
- ratify or revise the vertical-slice scenario;
- separately ratify or revise its milestone cuts;
- record a working codename only if useful.

Exit: WP-0002 has value-matching creator receipt claims, and no gameplay/content production begins from a recommendation the creator did not choose.

## M1 — Reversible engine spike and ugly constitutional toy

### M1A — Technical spike

Canonical packet: WP-0001.

- native Unity 6.3 LTS URP project on Apple Silicon;
- exact candidate editor/package/Xcode tuple and known-issues disposition;
- pure C# fixed-tick simulation shell;
- batch test/build command;
- versioned save round trip;
- Blender calibration asset through scripted export/import;
- representative slice-scale dynamic-building render test with per-instance faction/condition state, LODs, shadows, and probes;
- benchmark harness and captured report, without pretending the final city scale is known;
- pinned Draft 2020-12 schema validator with positive and adversarial save/pointer/packet/asset fixtures;
- Godot fallback assessment if any decisive gate fails.

Exit: D-0013 and D-0033 are ratified or superseded with evidence. The exact Unity patch is pinned only after Metal soak/recovery checks.

### M1B — Ugly constitutional toy

Canonical packet: WP-0002, dependent on accepted WP-0001 and the identity entry gate.

- compare the D-0030 city-grammar options at slice scale before generalizing a kernel;
- primitive city placement, one production bottleneck, and visible/aggregate logistics comparison;
- primitive road control under the creator-selected D-0007 path;
- two exact vehicle-module verbs;
- faction intent advances to the depot if the player waits;
- one city/road ownership transaction with save/retry recovery;
- typed human-cohort and robot-unit resident state plus human-only, robot-only, and mixed end-to-end loop smokes, kept orthogonal to the full preparation/module 2×2;
- a differentiated one-cohort/one-unit staffing/needs proof only if D-0039 is creator-bound to `MECHANICAL-COMPOSITIONS`;
- an explicit wait-without-departure faction claim and the creator-selected D-0008 clock-policy case;
- creator play evidence for both city comprehension and 30-second road-control viability.

Exit: D-0030 is ratified or superseded, both halves are viable enough to continue, and the ugly closed loop is more informative than the generalized framework it precedes.

### M1C — Trusted gatekeeper bootstrap

This workstream may be designed during M1A/M1B but cannot accept its own output. It is required before A2, not before the manually isolated A1 experiments.

- protected `main` and governance paths plus isolated agent credentials;
- protected event store deriving packet, receipt, lease, gate, and release state;
- full schema/semantic validation and immutable scenario/artifact registries;
- authenticated creator receipts bound to exact commit and artifact hashes;
- pairwise principal checks, hostile-gate tests, immutable backups, and a deliberate rollback canary.

Exit: every attack in `11-TRUST-AND-ENFORCEMENT.md` is rejected outside candidate write authority. Only creator acceptance can promote A1 to A2.

Agent pilot during M1: batch build/test automation and one bounded tooling or correctness packet may run at A1; neither may merge or promote autonomy automatically.

## M2 — Slice-specific simulation kernel

Deliverables:

- world clock and versioned counter/key-based randomness;
- stable ID registry and content manifest;
- resources, inventory, production, labor/capacity, and construction commands;
- one typed resident ledger whose authoritative membership derives human-only, robot-only, or mixed composition without a second editable truth;
- vehicle/cargo/module/expedition state;
- minimal faction memory and promises;
- crisis trigger/forecast/resolution;
- state hash, audit trace, new versioned section contracts/migrations as required, and golden saves without redefining accepted envelope/root v1;
- headless Last Bearing 2×2 with all four preparation/module cases, at least two viable cases, autonomous faction timing, and at least two city-changing returns;
- ongoing human-only, robot-only, and mixed loop-smoke/save regressions, plus differentiated mechanics only under an accepted D-0039 contract.

Exit: VS-0 paper/state proof passes all correctness and save gates.

## M3 — Graybox closed loop

Deliverables:

- one primitive city district with camera, placement, staffing, and logistics feedback;
- one primitive road corridor and selected control embodiment;
- two exact module verbs;
- persistent city/road ownership transaction with exact cargo, crew, time, damage, retry, and recovery behavior;
- depot encounter, autonomous faction action, doctrine-shaped city consequence, and two faction-memory behaviors;
- full UI skeleton, pause/speed, keyboard/controller path;
- resident/composition read models and UI that never assume a human is present;
- save at all critical transitions;
- external playtests across the preparation/module permutation matrix.
- human-only, robot-only, and mixed canonical-path regressions through city, road, return, and save; differentiated needs remain conditional on D-0039.

Exit: VS-1 proves city comprehension, 30-second road-control viability, causal coupling, systemic variation, faction autonomy, and recoverable saves.

Agent pilots during M2–M4 add, in order, one bounded correctness fix, one save migration fixture, and one quarantined asset. M6 remains the promotion audit rather than the first time the loop is exercised.

## M6 — Agent-loop pilot

Audit at least six bounded packets, including every failed or aborted attempt in the denominator:

1. a correctness defect;
2. a semantic-preserving performance optimization;
3. an additive save/content change;
4. an art asset through quarantine and lineup review;
5. a flagged small gameplay feature;
6. a deliberately failed canary/rollback drill.

Exit: independent verification, clean integration, evidence manifests, migration, and rollback all work. Promote autonomy only if the creator trusts the outputs.

## M7 — Founder playable

Provisional scope:

- 60–90 minutes of connected play;
- one settlement through two civic stages;
- two road corridors/POIs;
- two mechanically conflicting factions, even if the second has a small content footprint;
- one route/resource decision that affects both factions and proves triangulation rather than isolated reputation;
- one vehicle chassis with a small meaningful module set;
- workshop manufacturing with at least one physical batch/lot and one scheduled bilateral caravan trade; no global exchange yet;
- two crises and multiple recovery paths;
- complete new game, settings, save slots, load, failure/recovery, and end-of-build state.
- human-only, robot-only, and mixed colonies each complete the founder-playable loop and preserve their exact composition through saves; no supported composition depends on a hidden human. Mechanical differentiation remains conditional on the creator's D-0039 value.

Exit: the game supports repeat play and real balance observation rather than a single scripted slice.

## M7E — Manufacturing and Caravan Exchange proof

This is a dedicated bounded workstream after the founder loop proves that production, inventory, road ownership, factions, and saves are worth extending. It does not retroactively enlarge The Last Bearing.

Deliverables:

- tune and ratify one transparent aggregate world-population metric and threshold from campaign-scale evidence;
- define robot/human/cohort eligibility without creating a hidden mandatory-human gate;
- prove workshop jobs, batches, physical lots, grade/condition, split/merge lineage, civic reserves, and export policy;
- implement caravaner contact, certification, bonded storage, schedules, fees, access, sanctions, and visible institutional presence;
- prove one deterministic, physically backed regional matching model and one accepted unit of account or barter contract;
- stage, dispatch, delay, damage, lose, deliver, dispute, and settle shipments through exact-once custody/save transitions;
- close naked-order, infinite-buyer, self-match, retry-duplication, quality-laundering, and zero-cost arbitrage exploits;
- create the first caravan stockyard art/story proof under Texas iron × brutalist opera and tungsten over neon;
- register and pass the immutable Gate 7A scenario suite.

Exit: the exchange creates meaningful specialization and civic/faction/road consequences, remains physically conserved and explainable, survives saves and long runs, and is fun enough to justify production-scale content.

## M8 — Production alpha

Only after measured throughput and D-0031's human-time, budget, distribution, and pivot constraints are recorded should the project choose a 1.0 content envelope. A plausible but **not ratified** magnitude illustration is:

- one deeply evolving primary settlement rather than many colonies;
- three major factions plus minor groups;
- three vehicle chassis with 12–18 verb-changing modules;
- three or four authored regional road environments;
- roughly 25–35 functional building families with upgrade states;
- roughly 10–12 core resources/capacities and 20–30 legible recipes;
- a phased manufacturing and physical-goods trade economy, with the caravan exchange included only if M7E passes;
- 8–12 systemic crises plus authored encounters;
- a 12–20 hour first campaign with multiple civic/faction outcomes;
- continued play or a clear ending, to be ratified later.

This ceiling exists to expose magnitude, not to promise content before the slice measures production cost.

Exit: all constitutional systems exist, content is incomplete, campaign can run end-to-end, and saves migrate.

## M9 — Beta and release candidate

Focus:

- balance and economy exploit closure;
- save compatibility and recovery;
- performance across target Macs;
- onboarding, accessibility, settings, controller, localization readiness;
- asset consistency, audio mix, crash/soft-lock elimination;
- campaign pacing and faction consequence clarity;
- installer/signing/notarization/distribution decision;
- no uncontrolled feature growth.

Exit: complete content, locked feature surface, release-quality saves, and creator acceptance.

## What agents can scale—and what they cannot replace

Agents can scale tests, simulations, profiling, bounded code, content variations, Blender blockouts, validation, migration fixtures, QA paths, and documentation. They do not remove the need for:

- creator ratification of identity and tradeoffs;
- repeated real play;
- human art direction and taste;
- legal/provenance judgment;
- target-hardware observation;
- aggressive scope cuts.

The autonomous loop begins as a quality multiplier around a proven game. It is not a substitute for proving the game.
